RNIPP and FranceConnect:

Strategic Issues for Trans Refugees

This analysis presents the reasons why limited access to RNIPP file modifications for trans refugees recognized in France constitutes a major strategic challenge, both technically, legally, and in terms of human rights.

1. Technical Architecture of RNIPP and FranceConnect

1.1 The National Registry for Identification of Physical Persons (RNIPP)

The RNIPP constitutes a database of all physical persons born, living or having lived in France, and as such, the central digital identification infrastructure for State and related services:

Primary function: Centralized database containing the civil status of all persons born in France or having acquired French nationality or a residence permit, managed by INSEE.

Supplementary function: Authentication hub for users of State digital services, via FranceConnect, a secure digital identity service launched in 2018 to offer an alternative to multiple identifiers and passwords, and which is becoming the only means of connection to an increasing number of administrative procedures.

Data stored:

  • National Identification Number (NIR) - the "social security number"
  • Complete civil status: surname, first name(s), date and place of birth, sex, parentage
  • History of civil status modifications with timestamps
  • References to source civil status documents

Technical architecture:

  • Centralized system hosted by INSEE
  • Consultation API accessible to authorized organizations
  • Real-time synchronization with public services
  • Encryption of communications and stored data

1.2 FranceConnect: The State Authentication Hub

Operating principle:

FranceConnect functions as an Identity Broker using the OpenID Connect protocol:

  1. Authentication phase: The user authenticates with an approved identity provider (impots.gouv.fr, Ameli, La Poste Digital Identity, etc.)
  2. RNIPP verification: FranceConnect automatically queries the RNIPP to validate the match between declared identity and official identity
  3. Access token: If there's a match, issue a secure JWT token allowing access to the requested service
  4. Identity propagation: Transmission of verified identity data to the consuming service

RNIPP criticality in the ecosystem:

Without RNIPP match, access is systematically refused. There is no bypass mechanism or alternative validation.

Volume and criticality:

  • 40+ million active users (2024)
  • 37 million RNIPP consultations via FranceConnect in 2022
  • More than 1,000 connected public and private services
  • Growth of 20-30 million requests/year (excluding FranceConnect)

2. Ecosystem of Services Connected to FranceConnect

2.1 Critical Public Services

Tax administration:

  • impots.gouv.fr: IR and IS declarations, consultations, claims
  • Professional space: tax declarations for entrepreneurs

Health and social protection:

  • Ameli.fr: health insurance rights, care reimbursements, sick leave and benefits, work accident rights, disability pensions, European insurance card
  • Retirement space: simulation, liquidation requests
  • France Travail: unemployment rights, benefits and business creation aid, training and career change support
  • CAF: RSA, activity premium, family allowances, disability allowances, parental pensions
  • MSA: agricultural social benefits

Citizenship and rights:

  • ANTS: identity cards, passports, driving licenses
  • ANEF: residence permits, travel documents, access to French nationality
  • Service-public.fr: various administrative procedures

Education and training:

  • Mon compte formation (CPF): professional training
  • Parcoursup: higher education enrollment
  • Crous: student scholarships, housing

2.2 Extension to Private Sector

Banking and insurance services: Since 2021, FranceConnect+ allows access to private services contributing to public action: account opening, insurance subscriptions, mortgages.

Critical consequence: The inability to access FranceConnect leads to an increasingly unavoidable exclusion from digital counters allowing access to basic rights. The accelerated shift to all-digital, the reduction in non-digital accessible services, a pronounced trend toward eliminating conventional connection protocols considered less secure risk causing de facto exclusion from the entire French digital ecosystem, within less than 2 years.

3. RNIPP File Update Following Civil Status Modification

Although French legislation provides no difference in procedures for modifying civil status elements, whether first name or sex mention, between French nationals and refugees, the current functioning of RNIPP de facto creates a pronounced inequality between persons born in metropolitan France, DOM or certain COM on one hand and those born abroad or in New Caledonia on the other hand.

A person born abroad can be French, a person born in France can be a refugee, nevertheless the majority of refugees are born abroad and find themselves in the second situation.

Technical consequences:

  1. The FranceConnect system continues to search for the old identity in the RNIPP
  2. Access is systematically refused by the verification algorithm
  3. The error code displayed (Error Y190001: "Your session has expired or is invalid, close your browser tab and reconnect") in no way indicates the course of action to follow, nor even the real blocking factor, directing the user toward inappropriate technical solutions

Daily prejudice:

Impossibility of accessing vital digital services for 1-2 years, creating a situation of "prejudice" recognized by the administration itself.

4. The Surveillance File: Decree of December 19, 2023

4.1 Creation of a Discriminatory Registry

Official purpose: "Name and first name correspondence table" to facilitate administrative procedures.

Technical reality:

  • Systematic filing of any person who modified their civil status after 12/19/2023
  • 6-year retention of before/after modification data
  • Direct access granted to police services (first time in RNIPP history)
  • No possibility of opposition or rectification for concerned persons

Data collected:

  • Previous and subsequent surnames and first names
  • Date and place of birth
  • Declared sex
  • Date of change
  • Parentage

Disproportionate impact: In practice, this file mainly concerns:

  • Trans persons (first name/sex change)
  • Persons of foreign origin "Frenchifying" their name
  • Refugees (overrepresented in both categories)

4.2 Identified GDPR Violations

Article 5 - Processing principles:

  • Minimization: Excessive collection of unnecessary data
  • Purpose: Misuse of declared administrative purpose
  • Lawfulness: Insufficient legal basis for such intrusive filing

Article 9 - Sensitive data: Data on gender identity constitutes sensitive data requiring enhanced guarantees not respected.

Article 16 - Right of rectification: Practical impossibility of exercising this right faced with administrative inertia.

6. 4-Axis Parallel Action Strategy

Importance of a Coordinated Approach

To transform this systemic problem, it is essential to act simultaneously on four complementary axes, each reinforcing the others in a multiple leverage logic.

6.1 Legal Axis (priority)

Strategic group legal action:

Legal action represents the essential axis because only it can create the binding jurisprudence necessary to force administrations to adapt their systems. A group legal action would allow:

  • Pooling legal costs and expertise
  • Creating systemic pressure on several jurisdictions simultaneously
  • Obtaining principle decisions applicable to all similar situations
  • Mobilizing European law (GDPR, equal treatment directive) as transformation leverage

Necessary pre-litigation research:

Before launching the jurisdictional offensive, a thorough research phase is indispensable to:

  • Precisely map legal violations in each stage of the journey
  • Identify the most legally strategic cases
  • Build solid legal argumentation by type of jurisdiction
  • Quantify damages to optimize compensation requests

6.2 Technical Axis

Complete audit of OFPRA-RNIPP-social organizations interoperability:

  • Technical mapping of data flows between systems
  • Identification of breaking points in the transmission chain
  • Proposal of GDPR-compliant alternative architecture
  • Development of specifications for secure APIs

6.3 Social Axis

Experimentation and social innovation:

  • Training and professional monitoring for social workers
  • Communication of good practices to support associations
  • Experimentation of specialized digital support devices
  • Documentation of replicable social innovations

These axes will be articulated in our digital citizenship laboratory project and the first LGBTQIA+-focused Digital Relay in France.

6.4 Advocacy Axis

White paper of recommendations:

  • Concrete technical proposals for administrations
  • Regulatory and legislative recommendations
  • Benchmark of European good practices
  • Systemic transformation plan with calendar and indicators

Synergy of 4 Axes

This coordinated approach maximizes transformation chances because:

  • Legal axis creates legal obligation for change
  • Technical axis provides operational solutions
  • Social axis ensures appropriation by field actors
  • Advocacy axis facilitates institutional adoption

Group legal action remains the central strategic axis, as only it can compel administrations to abandon current discriminatory practices and adopt proposed technical solutions.

Conclusion

Limited access to RNIPP modification for trans refugees constitutes not just an administrative dysfunction, but a systemic lock that compromises effective exercise of their fundamental rights in an increasingly digitized society.

Resolving this problem represents a major strategic issue for:

  • Public action efficiency (reduction of manual processing costs)
  • Legal compliance (GDPR respect and European standards)
  • Political coherence (real equality before digital public service)
  • Technical innovation (inclusive and resilient architecture)
